# Fiction world intelligence and Arcanea naming architecture

Status: draft architecture and executable seed, 2026-07-18
Scope: Starlight Knowledge Tree. This document does not alter or promote Arcanea canon.

## The correction

`shadow-corrupted resonance` is an explanation, not a name. It stacks a source, a condition, and a technical substrate into one phrase. Nobody would claim it in dialogue. Nobody would fear it, wear it, mock it, or put it on a chapter title.

That failure appears elsewhere when the system translates an admired fictional idea directly into Arcanea's abstract vocabulary. The output remains semantically correct but loses identity. Common symptoms:

- adjective piles: `shadow-corrupted`, `Gate-touched`, `reality-shaping`;
- backend language escaping into story language: frequency, resonance, harmonic, substrate;
- repeated fantasy defaults: veil, weave, echo, nexus, ascendant, awakened, prism;
- institutions named by explanation: `Tower of Refracted Light` rather than a name people actually use;
- one register for everyone, from scholar to child to field operator;
- terminology that describes a power but does not create a social identity;
- literal trope translation instead of an Arcanean inversion.

Arcanea needs a language system, not a synonym generator.

## Two graphs, one firewall

The Starlight Knowledge Tree should own the comparative layer. Arcanea should own only Arcanea.

```text

             |

Starlight Fiction Atlas

             v

  explicit rejected expression
  one original inversion
             |

  naming forge

```

External franchise names can appear in research, search, comparison, and citation. They cannot become Arcanea class labels, canon entities, product brands, copied prose, or visual instructions.

## Name the thing, then explain it

Every important concept should support several registers. One cumbersome phrase should not do every job.

| Register | Purpose | Example shape |
|---|---|---|
| Canon | stable identity used by the world | `Luminor` |
| Formal | academy, law, medicine, doctrine | `spiritual severance` |
| Field | what specialists say under pressure | `Blackwake` (research candidate only) |
| Spoken | what ordinary people say | short, concrete, culturally variable |
| Interface | plain-language help text | `Shadow exposure` |

A field term may be sharp and partial. The formal term may be precise. The interface gloss may be boring on purpose. They should not be collapsed.

`Blackwake`, `Blackwake Arcan`, and `the Severance` in the seed are not canon recommendations. They demonstrate the correct naming shape and preserve their unresolved collision risks. `Blackwake Arcan` is a deliberately compact profession candidate: two words, a field image, and a native person noun. It exists to compete in dialogue tests, not to bypass canon review.

## Arcanea's lexical constitution

### Keep the strongest foundations

The best existing terms have a clean silhouette and room for meaning:

- Arcanea
- Lumina
- Nero
- Luminor
- Malachar
- Godbeast
- the Arc

They are compact enough to carry history without explaining themselves.

### Review the staging vocabulary

These terms may be useful internally but need a prestige pass before promotion:

- Gate-Touched
- Voidtouched
- Bonded
- Architects
- bending
- resonance
- harmonic energy
- prism bending
- code bending
- world bending

The issue is not that every word is forbidden. `Resonance` is accurate canon language. Its overuse makes unrelated systems sound generated from the same prompt. `Bending` is legible but carries a powerful modern franchise association when combined with element categories. A review must compare the complete bundle, not argue that one generic word is legally available.

### Use controlled scarcity

Reserve a small number of abstract words for the places where they have real cosmological force. If `resonance` explains geography, magic, emotion, materials, politics, AI, medicine, and corruption, it stops meaning anything.

Each major noun receives one primary domain. Secondary uses need a concrete modifier or a different register.

### Build word families

A strong coined root should survive grammatical use:

```text
noun       a named thing or force
person     someone who bears or practices it
verb       what the person actually does
adjective  a natural inflection, not a second invented word
slang      how speech wears the term down over time
```

Reject roots that only work as a lore heading.

### Vary linguistic register with intent

Arcanea should not manufacture every term from the same pseudo-Latin or compound-English machine. Its cultures can draw from different sound systems, but each system needs documented phonotactics and history. Variety must come from cultures, migration, institutions, and class, not random syllables.

For every naming family, record:

- language or dialect of origin;
- sound inventory and stress pattern;
- literal root meaning;
- historical semantic change;
- who uses the term and who refuses it;
- formal, field, spoken, and insulting forms;
- plural and derived forms;
- collision and derivative-distance review.

No fake etymology should be added after a name is chosen merely to make it look deep.

## Prestige name gate

A candidate receives 0–5 on each axis:

1. mouthfeel;
2. semantic charge;
3. role clarity;
4. distinctiveness;
5. inflection range;
6. derivative distance.

A name fails before scoring if it:

- requires a sentence to become interesting;
- visibly translates a famous franchise term;
- repeats the estate's exhausted fantasy vocabulary without new meaning;
- confuses a person, condition, discipline, institution, and rank;
- resembles an existing franchise through a distinctive bundle;
- has no spoken form;
- contradicts locked Arcanea cosmology;
- lacks preliminary search and trademark clearance before public lock.

The executable `auditPrestigeName()` function catches mechanical warning signs. It is a filter, not a taste oracle. Human read-aloud judgment and cultural review remain mandatory.

## Story architecture captured by the graph

The Atlas maps why a fictional system works, not only what it contains.

Each Pattern Card records:

- the human desire it serves;
- its social machinery;
- its progression loop;
- its cost structure;
- the character decisions it pressures;
- works that demonstrate it;
- source-specific expression that must be excluded;
- known failures and overused versions.

The first seed isolates three patterns:

- hazard as profession;
- school as belonging machine;
- reciprocal sovereign bond.

## Benchmarking without imitation

"Better" cannot mean copying whatever currently wins attention. Each system is tested against explicit dimensions, with evidence attached:

- spoken identity;
- linguistic depth;
- character choice pressure;
- social embedding;
- progression payoff;
- transmedia memory.

No franchise wins every dimension. The graph can record that one world excels at immediate class identity while another handles institutions, progression, moral pressure, or creature relationships more convincingly. Arcanea then targets the strongest known standard on each axis and must still produce its own structural inversion.

A benchmark result is not a model's confidence score. It needs receipts: read-aloud lines, pressure scenes, stakeholder consequences, threshold deltas, source citations, and derivative-distance review. This turns comparative taste into something agents can inspect and improve instead of a prompt that says "make it more epic."

This is more useful than storing copied biographies or plot summaries. It lets Arcanea compare institutions, power ecologies, character engines, progression, emotional promises, and moral pressure across hundreds of worlds.

## The translation contract

Every cross-world translation must contain:

- named external references with provenance;
- original Pattern Cards rather than copied lore;
- at least three Arcanea anchors;
- retained human intent;
- rejected source expression;
- one structural inversion that changes the moral or dramatic logic;
- explicit `research_only`, `staging`, or `locked` status.

A request for a "jujutsu sorcerer in Arcanea" should not produce `Shadow Resonance Sorcerer`. That is the exact failure this system forbids.

The seed instead keeps specialist hazard work and individualized embodied practice, then changes the premise: Arcanean Shadow is Void stripped of Spirit. Restoration of agency can therefore be a higher achievement than extermination. The resulting profession, methods, slang, institutions, and character conflicts must be named from that Arcanean premise.

A request for a Hogwarts-like experience keeps school belonging and visible growth. It rejects sorting, points, spells, sports, architecture, and personality-box Houses. Arcanean House membership can change through practice, refuge, debt, politics, or Gate development.

A request for an Eragon-like bond keeps exceptional partnership and shared consequence. It rejects copied bond rules, language magic, orders, artifacts, and history. The Arcanean dragon can refuse the mission or leave the institution. The dragon remains a sovereign character.

## Rights and provenance

The graph uses these tiers:

- `arcanea_original`
- `public_domain`
- `cc0_metadata`
- `open_licensed`
- `editorial_reference`
- `partner_licensed`
- `restricted`

Modern protected works default to `editorial_reference`. Store factual identification, official source URLs, rights notes, and original analysis. Do not store copied wiki prose, dialogue, maps, character art, spell lists, extensive plot retellings, or source-styled generation prompts.

Every source record carries a source title, evidence type, verification status, access timestamp, rights-review date, license scope, and review reference. `metadata_only` is deliberately honest: a syntactically valid URL is not evidence that its contents or rights have been reviewed. Promotion to `content_reviewed` or `rights_reviewed` requires a dated evidence trail.

Traditional, religious, and living cultural material needs a separate cultural-tradition namespace. Legal availability does not remove the need for context, attribution, and primary readers from the relevant communities.

## Files in this proof

- `data/schemas/fiction-world.schema.json` mirrors the structural record model; runtime validation adds cross-record reference and naming-policy checks.
- `packages/graph-schema/src/fiction-world.ts` provides Zod contracts.
- `packages/graph-utils/src/fiction-world.ts` validates Atlas data and audits mechanical naming failures.
- `data/fiction-worlds/atlas.seed.json` contains three rights-aware reference records, three original Pattern Cards, six evidence-oriented benchmark dimensions, three Arcanea translations, and four research-only naming candidates.

The seed is deliberately small. Scale should come from verified sources and good Pattern Cards, not bulk lore scraping.
